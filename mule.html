We Lost A Good Friend

The life and rememberance of the town Mule.

Posted by Joe Starks on August 24, 1887

A Good Friend.

Our’se town has lost its greatest ass’t today. Dis mule dat we bury heah today lived a tougha life, and it is wit a heavy heart dat we lays it down fuh rest.

Born aftuh Matt Bonner confused a donkey for ‘is wife, the scrawny beast lived inuh slavery to ‘is father. Barely even fed, Mr. Mule Jr. suff’ed a mighty tough life ‘til I recognized dat he was a smartuh dan ‘is master, and I set ‘im free.

Mr. Mule Jr. will be remembered by Lindsay who served him coffee, Mrs. Pearson who’s tought he was her husband, and g’old Becky for sharin comp’ny. I ‘ope his father is proud cause dis’ll be duh only son he’ll ever git, and dey look so close Mrs. Bonner thought they’se was twins.

Mr. Mule Jr. woulda never wanted some sad funeral, so I say let’s start the feast in der’se honor.

May He Forever Roam Plentiful Fields Of Grain.

To go places and do things that have never been done before – that’s what living is all about.
